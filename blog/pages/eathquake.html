A trigger word detection model, as suggested by the name, aims to detect trigger words such as 'Hey Siri!'. The audio is continously listened and when the waveform suggests the last waves might have been formed by the trigger word the model outputs a higher value.

In this project, we tried to see how well Andrew Ng's trigger-word-detection model would perform for predicting earthquakes. We applied the mentioned logic to see if preceeding waves give information about the time to failure.

Turns out it was not the best model for this problem, but we have learnt a lot during the process. It was my first Deep Learning project, and we worked with a really big dataset (of around 610 million points).


See our kaggle notebook for the implementation.
